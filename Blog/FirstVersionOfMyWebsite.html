First Version of my Website? Completed it mate.

Web development, August 13th 2020

Here I am. A fully functioning, well-designed website available for the world to site. Created by me. It’s a feeling that’s hard to describe, but I’ll do my best to.

Beginning this project, I can’t say I exactly knew what my end goal was. I’d seen some examples of nicely designed personal websites online, and there were features of these that not only did I want to emulate, I also simply wanted to learn how to code.

One personal website that significantly inspired my creation belongs to Gary Sheng of www.garysheng.com. It was a chic, modern-looking site that he’d programmed himself. The fact that he was also a software developer is perhaps something that drew me to it. His website isn’t the most complex or sprawling; it’s simple. It felt attainable whilst being of a level that I’d be proud to have created.

Right at the beginning of this project I had discovered and practiced using CSS Grid. This style gave me to flexibility I needed to have my website work both on my 13” laptop and my 25” monitor. I was able to create pages that were responsive on a wide range of desktop screens, if not yet usable on mobile or tablet.

For me though, that’s okay. I have only been learning web development for less than 2 weeks, and it’s important for me that I don’t get hung up on my perfectionism. I am understanding that at this time I just don’t have the skills or knowledge required to create my perfect website, and I’m still happy because I can see the progress in front of my eyes.

I decided that including a personal blog on the site would be beneficial for me. Not only is it an added level of complexity to aid me on my learning curve, it can also become a space for me to open up and talk. I’m not the strongest communicator, especially when discussing personal matters, and my girlfriend has long suggested that a written journal would help with that.

I see myself as a strong writer, but I often rejected her idea as I saw nothing to write about. Now, I have a journey to detail and a story to share, and I can do it all in the moment. I see it as a positive feedback loop where I expand my programming skillset by designing myself a platform in which I can then improve my ability to express myself. This in turn fuels more desire to program.

My journey is just beginning, as I learn new skills that help improve my website and transform it into my dream platform.

My next steps? Learning responsive web design to bring this website to users on mobile as well as devices of all sizes. My early research points to Bootstrap 4 being the best system for achieving this goal, but I remain open to new ideas.
